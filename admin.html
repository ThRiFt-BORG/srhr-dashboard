📖 Complete Guide — How to Update the Dashboard

Step-by-step instructions for non-technical staff. No coding required.
The single rule: Never edit code files. Policy status changes go through the Google Sheet (restricted to approved staff). Field updates/reports go through the public Updates Form — anyone on the team can submit one, no sheet access needed.

A. How to Update a Policy Status or Implementation %

1
Open the EACHRights SRHR Data Google Sheet (ask your supervisor for the link if you don't have it).
2
Click the Policies tab at the bottom of the sheet.
3
Find the row for your county and policy. Use Ctrl+F (or Cmd+F on Mac) to search by policy name or county.
4
Update the relevant cell(s):
  • status column — change to one of: Adopted, Enacted, In Progress, Draft, Stalled, Not Operational
  • impl_pct column — enter a number from 0 to 100 (no % sign)
  • gap column — update the key gap text
5
Google Sheets saves automatically. No need to click Save.
6
Go to the portal and open the county page. Refresh the page (F5) — the updated data will appear within seconds.
⚠️ Do not change the county_id or policy_id columns — these are used to match data and must stay exactly as they are.

B. How to Add a Field Update / Report

1
Go to any county page and click "+ Add Update" (or open the form link directly — ask your supervisor if you don't have it).
2
Fill in the form: County, Date of Event, Update Title, Description, Source/Organization, and optional Tags.
3
Submit. The form writes directly into the Updates sheet — no manual sheet editing, no risk of typos in county names since it's a dropdown.
4
Refresh the county page on the portal — your update appears in the Updates feed within seconds.
Why a form instead of direct sheet access? It keeps the sheet's edit access restricted to a small trusted group, while still letting any field staff submit updates safely.

C. How to Attach a Policy Document

1
Upload your PDF to Google Drive. Right-click the file → Share → Anyone with the link can view → Copy link.
2
Open the Google Sheet → Policies tab → find the row for that policy.
3
Paste the Google Drive link into the doc_url column for that row.
4
Refresh the county page — a ⬇ Download button will appear next to that policy automatically.

D. What Updates Immediately vs. What Requires Developer Help

✅ You can update yourself (via Google Sheet):

  • Policy status (e.g. change "In Progress" to "Adopted")
  • Implementation percentage (e.g. change 0% to 45%)
  • Key gap text
  • Document links
  • Field updates and reports (via the Updates Form — no sheet access needed)

🔧 Requires developer to update in counties.js:

  • Adding a brand new policy that doesn't exist yet
  • Changing county statistics (population, maternal mortality, budget figures)
  • Adding or removing advocacy priorities
  • Changing stakeholder lists
  • Adding a new county
📧 For developer updates, send Dev the new data with clear instructions on what to change. He updates counties.js, commits to GitHub, and changes go live within minutes.

E. Troubleshooting

My changes in the sheet are not showing on the portal

1
Hard refresh the page: press Ctrl+Shift+R (Windows) or Cmd+Shift+R (Mac).
2
Come to Admin Panel → Google Sheets → click Force Refresh from Sheet.
3
Check that county_id and policy_id are spelled exactly correctly with no extra spaces.

The status dot shows red / Not Connected

1
Check your internet connection.
2
Make sure the Google Sheet is still published: File → Share → Publish to web — it should say "This document is published to the web".
3
Contact Peter if the problem persists.
